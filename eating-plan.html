The hand portion method is a practical, portable way to eat better without obsessing over numbers. Your own hand is your guide — sized perfectly for your body.

THE FOUR MEASURES
One for each macronutrient. Use them at every meal.
🤚
Protein — Build Every Meal Here First
YOUR PALM
Same thickness & width as your palm (not fingers)
≈ 20–30g protein · 85–115g cooked · 3–4oz

Protein is the foundation. It keeps you full, preserves muscle and supports fat loss. Always put your protein on the plate first — everything else builds around it.

Chicken breastSalmon filletLean beef minceTurkey minceTuna steakCod / haddockTinned tunaPrawnsSirloin steak2–3 whole eggsGreek yogurt (1 cup)Cottage cheese (1 cup)TofuEdamame
✊
Vegetables — Eat As Much As You Like
CLOSED FIST
Same size as your clenched fist
≈ 1 cup · ~100g · Minimal calories · High fibre

Non-starchy veg is your best friend. It fills you up, fuels your gut, and adds vitamins and minerals without blowing your calories. Pile it on — you genuinely can't overdo it.

BroccoliSpinachKaleMixed salad leavesPeppersCourgetteCucumberGreen beansAsparagusCauliflowerMushroomsTomatoesCabbageBrussels sproutsPak choiSugar snap peas
🤲
Carbohydrates — Fuel Around Your Training
CUPPED HAND
Fingers together, palm cupped into a bowl
≈ 20–30g carbs · 100–130g cooked · ½–⅔ cup

Carbs give you energy to train and recover. More on heavy training days, less on rest days. Go for whole grain options where you can — they digest slower and keep you fuller.

Basmati riceBrown ricePastaSweet potatoWhite potatoOats / porridgeQuinoaCouscousWholegrain bread (1 slice)LentilsChickpeasKidney beansBananaAppleBerries (2 handfuls)
👍
Fats — Essential But Portion Carefully
YOUR THUMB
From the tip to the second joint
≈ 7–12g fat · ~1 tbsp · Calories add up fast here

Healthy fats are essential for hormones, joints, and brain health — but they are calorie-dense. One thumb per meal is enough. This is where people most often accidentally overeat.

Olive oilAvocado (¼)Peanut butterAlmond butterMixed nutsAlmondsCheddar cheeseFeta cheeseButterCoconut oilSesame oilSunflower seedsDark choc (2 squares)

MEAL IDEAS
Real examples using palm · fist · cupped hand · thumb.
Breakfast
Start Strong — Protein First
🤚 Protein✊ Veg🤲 Carbs👍 Fats
Eggs & Oats — Scrambled eggs on wholegrain toast with wilted spinach. Small bowl of oats with berries on the side.
Veggie Omelette — 3-egg omelette with mushrooms, peppers and feta. Slice of wholegrain toast or small portion of oats.
Yogurt Bowl — Big cup of Greek yogurt, mixed berries, mixed nuts, handful of spinach or cucumber alongside.
Protein Smoothie — Protein powder + milk, banana, peanut butter, handful of spinach blended in.
💡 A high-protein breakfast dramatically reduces mid-morning hunger and cuts total daily calorie intake.
Lunch
Midday Fuel
🤚 Protein✊ Veg🤲 Carbs👍 Fats
Chicken Rice Bowl — Grilled chicken breast, basmati rice, mixed salad, drizzle of olive oil and lemon.
Tuna Pasta Salad — Tinned tuna, wholegrain pasta, cucumber & tomato, avocado quarter.
Turkey Wrap — Sliced turkey in a wholegrain wrap, mixed salad, hummus. Fruit on the side for carbs.
Salmon Salad Bowl — Tinned or fresh salmon, quinoa, big salad base, olive oil dressing.
💡 Batch cook rice or quinoa on Sunday — hitting your lunch portions becomes effortless all week.
Dinner
Evening Meal
🤚 Protein✊ Veg🤲 Carbs👍 Fats
Salmon & Sweet Potato — Baked salmon, roasted sweet potato wedges, steamed broccoli, drizzle of olive oil.
Beef Mince Chilli — Lean mince with kidney beans, brown rice, side of greens. Great for batch cooking.
Turkey Stir Fry — Turkey strips, mixed veg, sesame oil, rice noodles or egg fried rice.
Baked Cod & Veg — White fish with Mediterranean veg, served with couscous or potato.
💡 On rest days drop to one cupped hand of carbs. On heavy training days go up to two.
Snacks
Smart Snacking
🤚 Protein🤲 Carbs👍 Fats
Greek Yogurt + Fruit — Cup of Greek yogurt, berries or banana, small drizzle of peanut butter.
Boiled Eggs + Apple — 2 boiled eggs, apple slices. Easy to prep and grab on the go.
Cottage Cheese + Pineapple — High protein, natural carb, very low calorie compared to how filling it is.
💡 If genuinely hungry between meals, always snack on something with protein first.
Post-Training
Recovery Window
🤚🤚 2× Protein🤲🤲 2× Carbs
After training, prioritise protein and carbs. Drop fats in this meal — fat slows absorption and you want nutrients reaching your muscles fast.
Shake + Banana — Protein shake post-session, banana on the way home. Simple, fast, effective.
Chicken & White Rice — White rice (faster digesting than brown post-training), double palm of chicken. Light on the oil.
💡 Aim to eat within 60–90 minutes of finishing your session.
