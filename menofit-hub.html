Exercise at Home
Exercise at Home
Simple movements to complement your MenoFit sessions.
No gym needed — just your body and a resistance band.
🌸 How to use these exercises
These movements are designed to sit between your MenoFit sessions — not replace them. 2–3 times per week is plenty. Focus on quality of movement over speed. If anything causes pain, stop and let Ritchie know.
🦵
Lower Body
Glutes, quads, hamstrings
›
Bodyweight Squat
Feet shoulder-width apart. Lower as if sitting into a chair, chest up, knees tracking over toes. Drive through heels to stand.
3 × 12No equipmentQuads · Glutes
Glute Bridge
Lie on back, knees bent. Drive hips up, squeeze glutes at the top, hold 2 seconds. Lower slowly. Add a resistance band above knees to progress.
3 × 15No equipmentBand optionGlutes · Hamstrings
Reverse Lunge
Step one foot back and lower your back knee toward the floor. Keep front knee above ankle. Push through front heel to return. Alternate legs.
3 × 10 each legNo equipmentQuads · Balance
Wall Sit
Back flat against wall, slide down until thighs parallel to floor. Hold. Great for leg endurance and bone loading through the femur.
3 × 30–45 secNo equipmentQuads · Bone Health
Lateral Band Walk
Band above knees or ankles. Step sideways in a squat position, maintaining tension in the band throughout. Don't let feet come fully together.
3 × 15 each wayBand requiredGlutes · Hips
💪
Upper Body
Shoulders, back, arms
›
Wall Press-Up
Hands at chest height on wall. Bend elbows and bring chest toward wall, then push back. Easier than floor press-ups — ideal starting point.
3 × 12No equipmentChest · Shoulders
Band Pull-Apart
Hold band at shoulder height, arms straight. Pull apart by squeezing shoulder blades together. Control the return. Excellent for posture.
3 × 15Band requiredUpper back · Posture
Overhead Press (Band)
Stand on band centre, hold ends at shoulder height. Press both hands overhead until arms straight. Lower slowly.
3 × 10Band requiredShoulders · Triceps
Bent-Over Row (Band)
Stand on band, hinge forward, back flat. Pull ends toward hips, elbows driving back. Squeeze shoulder blades at the top.
3 × 12Band requiredBack · Biceps

Core
Stability from the inside out
›
⚠️ Important
Avoid high-impact jumping and heavy breath-holding. Focus on controlled movements and always breathe throughout. If you have any pelvic floor concerns, speak to Ritchie.
Dead Bug
Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°. Slowly lower one arm behind head while extending the opposite leg. Keep lower back pressed to floor throughout.
3 × 8 each sideNo equipmentDeep core
Modified Plank
Forearms and knees on floor, body in a straight line. Hold without letting hips drop or rise. Breathe steadily throughout.
3 × 20–30 secNo equipmentFull core
Bird Dog
On hands and knees — extend one arm forward and the opposite leg back. Hold 2 seconds. Return and alternate. Keep hips level.
3 × 10 each sideNo equipmentCore · Balance

Mobility & Flexibility
Move better, recover faster
›
🌸 Why this matters
Falling oestrogen affects joint lubrication and connective tissue. Regular gentle mobility work reduces stiffness, improves posture and supports recovery between sessions.
Hip Flexor Stretch
Kneel on one knee, other foot forward. Push hips forward gently until you feel a stretch at the front of the kneeling hip. Hold.
30–45 sec each sideHips · Posture
Cat-Cow
On hands and knees. Alternate between arching back upward (cat) and letting it dip (cow). Move slowly and breathe with each movement.
10 slow repsSpine · Back
Chest Opener
Clasp hands behind your back. Squeeze shoulder blades together and open chest toward ceiling. Counteracts forward posture from desk work.
3 × 20–30 secChest · Posture
Standing Hamstring Stretch
One foot on a low step, leg straight. Hinge gently forward from hips until you feel a stretch down the back of the thigh. Don't round your back.
30 sec each sideHamstrings

Nutrition
Nutrition Basics
Simple, evidence-informed guidance.
No diets. No rules. Just what works.
🌸 The MenoFit approach
This isn't a diet plan. It's practical guidance based on what research shows actually helps during the menopause transition. Focus on adding good things, not cutting everything out.
🥩
Protein — Most Important
Muscle, metabolism, fullness
›
Muscle mass naturally declines during menopause as oestrogen falls. Adequate protein combined with resistance training like MenoFit is the most effective way to maintain muscle and support your metabolism.
1.6–2g
per kg bodyweight daily
25–35g
per meal target
✅ Good sources
Chicken, turkey, fish, eggs, Greek yoghurt, cottage cheese, legumes, tofu. Aim for a palm-sized portion at each meal.
⚡ Practical tip
If you struggle to hit protein through food alone, a plain whey or plant-based protein shake is a convenient tool — not a shortcut.
🦴
Bone Health
Calcium & Vitamin D
›
Bone density drops significantly during menopause. Nutrition and resistance training are the two most powerful non-medical tools available.
700mg
calcium per day
10μg
vitamin D daily
🥛 Calcium sources
Dairy, fortified plant milks, tinned sardines/salmon with bones, dark leafy greens (kale, broccoli), almonds, tofu made with calcium sulphate.
☀️ Vitamin D in the UK
Between October and March, UK sunlight isn't strong enough to produce vitamin D. The NHS recommends a 10μg supplement daily through autumn and winter. Speak to your GP if concerned about levels.
⚖️
Weight & Metabolism
Why it changes and what helps
›
🌸 It's not your fault
Weight gain around the middle during menopause is driven by hormonal changes, not willpower. Oestrogen shifts fat storage from hips and thighs to the abdomen. Understanding this matters because the approach needs to change too.
✅ What actually helps
Resistance training to maintain muscle and metabolic rate. Adequate protein. Balanced meals with protein, fat and fibre rather than high-carb spikes. Consistent sleep. Stress management.
⚠️ What doesn't help
Very low calorie dieting — this accelerates muscle loss and slows metabolism. Cutting entire food groups. Relying on cardio alone without resistance training.
💧
Hydration
Often overlooked, always important
›
Hot flushes cause fluid loss. Dehydration worsens fatigue, brain fog and joint stiffness — symptoms already heightened during menopause.
1.5–2L
water per day
+500ml
on exercise days
💧 Simple habit
Start every morning with a large glass of water before anything else. Pale yellow urine means well hydrated. Dark yellow means drink more.

Lifestyle & Symptoms
Understanding what's happening and practical ways to manage it.
😴
Sleep
Why it's disrupted and how to help it
›
🌸 Why sleep suffers
Night sweats, anxiety and hormonal changes disrupt sleep during menopause. Poor sleep increases cortisol, promotes fat storage, reduces muscle recovery and worsens other symptoms. Sleep is not a luxury.
✅ What helps
Keep bedroom cool (16–18°C ideal). Consistent sleep and wake times — even weekends. Reduce screens 60 minutes before bed. Avoid caffeine after 2pm. Light evening movement is better than intense late-night exercise. If night sweats are severe, speak to your GP about HRT.
🧠
Stress & Cortisol
The hidden driver of symptoms
›
Chronic stress raises cortisol, which worsens menopause symptoms, promotes abdominal fat storage, disrupts sleep and increases hot flush frequency.
⚡ The exercise connection
Resistance training like MenoFit reduces cortisol over time. The short-term stress of a workout is followed by a recovery drop that brings cortisol lower than before — one reason why regular training is so effective for symptom management.
✅ Simple strategies
Daily walks outdoors. Slow breathing (4 seconds in, 6 out) activates your parasympathetic nervous system. Protect time for things you enjoy. Social connection — which is exactly what the MenoFit group provides.
🌡️
Common Symptoms
What's normal, what helps, when to act
›
🔥 Hot flushes & night sweats
Experienced by around 75% of women. Exercise reduces frequency and severity over time. Managing triggers (caffeine, alcohol, spicy food) helps day to day. HRT is the most effective medical treatment.
🧠 Brain fog
Very common and often distressing. Regular exercise, sleep, hydration and stable blood sugar all help. Usually improves post-menopause. You are not losing your mind — this is hormonal and temporary.
💙 Mood changes
Anxiety, low mood and irritability are hormonally driven. Exercise is clinically shown to improve mood. Social exercise like MenoFit has an additional benefit over exercising alone.
⚠️ When to see your GP
If symptoms are significantly affecting your quality of life, work or relationships — please speak to your GP. HRT is safe for most women and highly effective. You don't have to just manage through.

Your Session Guide
What we do, why we do it, and how to get the most from it.
🌸 Why MenoFit is built the way it is
Every element is deliberately chosen based on what the evidence shows works specifically for menopausal women. It's not a standard fitness class adapted for older women — it's built from scratch around your needs.
🗂️
Session Structure
What happens and why
›
🔥 Warm Up
Pulse raiser and joint mobility. Raises core temperature, increases blood flow, reduces injury risk. Never skipped.
🏋️ Compound Movement
A major multi-joint exercise. Compounds recruit the most muscle and provide the greatest bone loading — critical for bone density maintenance.
🦵 Legs 1 & 2
Two sets of lower body work targeting different muscle groups. Lower body loading is particularly important for bone density in the hip and spine.
💪 Upper Body 1 & 2
Pulling and pushing movements. Supports posture and maintains shoulder and back function as we age.
🏁 Finishers
Short focused bursts targeting specific muscles. Adds intensity without excessive fatigue. Often core, glutes or shoulder stability.
🧘 Cool Down & Stretches
Brings heart rate down gradually. The shift here helps reduce cortisol after the session and begins the recovery process.
💡
Getting the Most from Sessions
Practical tips for progress
›
✅ Always tell Ritchie
Any pain, injury, change in medication or new diagnosis. Sessions can always be adapted. Progress within your capacity — not through pain.
📈 Progressive overload
To keep getting stronger the challenge needs to gradually increase. This doesn't always mean heavier — more reps, better technique, less rest or greater range all count. Trust the process.
🍌 On session days
Eat something with protein and carbs 1–2 hours before. Bring water. Comfortable supportive footwear. Give yourself time to arrive without rushing.
💜 Recovery between sessions
Muscle is built during rest, not during exercise. Sleep, protein and managing stress between sessions are as important as the sessions themselves.

About MenoFit
About MenoFit
The programme, the philosophy, the person behind it.
🌸 What is MenoFit?
MenoFit is a specialist 6-week strength and fitness programme designed specifically for women going through perimenopause and menopause. Every session is built around what your body needs at this stage of life — not a generic fitness class adapted for older women.
💜 The evidence behind it
The programme is built on research showing that resistance training twice per week is the most effective form of exercise for menopausal women — improving bone density, maintaining muscle mass, reducing hot flush frequency, supporting mood and managing weight. MenoFit delivers exactly that in a supportive group environment.
✅ Why the group format works
Research consistently shows that social exercise produces better outcomes than exercising alone for this demographic. Accountability, shared experience and peer support all contribute. The women in your MenoFit cohort are going through similar experiences — that shared understanding matters.
👤 About Ritchie
Ritchie is a certified personal trainer with 8 years of experience, a qualified menopause fitness specialist, and a GLP-1 informed PT. He works at an Everyone Active leisure centre in the UK and runs MenoFit because he believes in it — not for the numbers. Having lost 5 stone himself through fitness and lifestyle change, he understands the process from both sides.
⚠️ Important
MenoFit is a fitness programme, not a medical service. The guidance in this hub is general and informational. Always speak to your GP about symptoms, treatment options and any changes to your health. Ritchie works alongside your medical care — not instead of it.
